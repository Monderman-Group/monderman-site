Structural Clarity Snapshot

A guided four-step diagnostic for one workflow or operating slice. Use it to estimate likely structural overhead and surface the design conditions most likely creating it.

Use note: Do not enter confidential, classified, regulated, or personally sensitive information. This tool provides directional estimates, not audited organizational values.

Step 1 — Define the workflow

Start with one bounded workflow. Keep it specific enough that one team, division, or operating slice could clearly recognize it.

Step 2 — Rate structural clarity

These questions focus on whether the workflow is legible in practice. Lower clarity generally means higher structural overhead.

Ownership Clarity

How clear is ownership for this workflow?

Authority Clarity

How clear is decision authority?

Handoff Clarity

How explicit are the handoffs between people or teams?

Review Logic

How coherent is the review and approval structure?

Step 3 — Mark the most common friction signals

Select the friction patterns that most often show up in the workflow you are assessing.

Step 4 — Estimated structural overhead

These estimates are directional and based on your selected scope, headcount, burden inputs, and generalized labor-rate assumptions. They are intended to surface likely administrative overhead, not provide audited financial values.
