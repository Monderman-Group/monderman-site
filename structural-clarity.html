Structural Clarity Diagnostic

A focused diagnostic for surfacing where authority, ownership, and accountability have drifted apart.

What it surfaces

Ownership ambiguity, role overlap, escalation confusion, accountability diffusion, and dependence on informal influence.

What you get back

A Structural Clarity Score, directional benchmark comparison, ambiguity charts, boundary-risk signals, and a narrative interpretation layer.

Diagnostic Input

Assess one team, role boundary, or operating area.

Use the guided diagnostic for the clearest experience, or upload a template-compatible CSV if you already have structured organizational data.

Step 1 · Structural context

Describe the team, role boundary, or operating area where clarity may be eroding.

This note is optional. Keep it short and non-sensitive.

This tool works best when focused on one organizational boundary at a time.

Step 2 · Ownership clarity

This section measures whether ownership is explicit, stable, and actually recognized by the people involved.

This tool is not measuring decision speed; it is measuring whether ownership itself is structurally clear.

Step 3 · Authority and escalation

This section examines whether formal authority and actual decision rights line up in practice.

If clarity depends mostly on personalities, the structure is usually less clear than it appears.

Step 4 · Role boundaries and accountability

This section measures overlap, diffusion, and whether accountability survives handoffs and shared work.

Boundary conflict is often a structural signal, not just a people problem.

Step 5 · Optional signals and privacy

These optional fields improve directional estimates of structural drag and coordination burden.

Privacy notice

Monderman does not store, retain, sell, or share diagnostic input beyond what is required to generate the immediate readout. Information submitted through this prototype may be processed temporarily by secure third-party AI infrastructure to generate narrative output. Do not enter classified, proprietary, regulated, or personally sensitive information.

Do not enter sensitive or proprietary details; a short organizational description is enough.

This diagnostic is directional. It is designed to produce a credible first read on structural ambiguity, not an audited organizational design assessment.

      How this works

      This diagnostic evaluates one structural boundary at a time. It translates signals about ownership, authority, and accountability into a directional readout of structural clarity.

      Best use cases

      • Cross-functional teams
      • Program or matrix structures
      • Shared-service functions
      • Oversight and governance groups
      • Recurring ownership disputes between units

      What this is not

      • Not a decision-speed tool
      • Not a morale survey
      • Not a personality assessment
      • Not a replacement for org-design work

      What to trust

      Trust the structural pattern, not false precision. The strongest value here is surfacing where ownership, authority, and accountability no longer align cleanly.
