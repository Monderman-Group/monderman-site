How procurement works

Built to clear approval without a six-month cycle.

Monderman is designed to be straightforward to buy and easy to justify — transparent pricing, predictable annual cost, and a data posture suited to sensitive environments.

Published, predictable pricing

List pricing is on this page. No per-seat creep, no usage surprises, no quote required to see what a plan costs. Annual cost is fixed at signing.

Diagnostic data stays in your environment

The platform is built for institutions handling sensitive information. Diagnostic inputs and reads remain under your control; the assumption set behind every score is published, not hidden.

Single-vendor simplicity

One annual subscription, one line item, one renewal. Engagements are fixed-fee and scoped up front, so finance sees the full commitment before it starts.

Justification, built in

Every read estimates the fully-burdened cost of the drag it measures — the figure your approval memo needs, generated from your own baseline rather than a vendor claim.

For contractors operating under FAR. The Baseline plan is deliberately priced below the federal micro-purchase threshold, so a unit lead can establish a measured baseline as a discretionary purchase — before committing to a competitive procurement for a wider deployment.
For defense and government contractors

Overhead competitiveness is a structural condition. Measure it.

For organizations that bid and deliver against the government, how efficiently you run delivery and overhead shows up directly in two places: how competitive your proposals are, and how much margin survives execution. Administrative drag erodes both — quietly, and faster than most cost models capture.

Monderman measures and governs that drag inside your own organization, repeatedly and across perspectives, so you can bid leaner and protect margin without cutting the capability you actually deliver. It is not a cost-pass-through story; it is a read on the institutional conditions that determine whether you stay competitive as you scale.

Start contained

Most institutions begin with a single business unit on the Baseline plan, establish a measured baseline, and expand once the read has earned trust internally. That is the intended path — start where the evidence is cheap to gather, then scale on what you find.
