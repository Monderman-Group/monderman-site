Institutional Performance Snapshot

A guided four-step diagnostic for one bounded operating environment. It produces an overall performance score and four sub-scores: execution coherence, decision reliability, adaptive capacity, and institutional confidence.

Use note: Do not enter confidential, classified, regulated, or personally sensitive information. This tool provides directional estimates, not audited organizational values.

Step 1 — Define the operating context

Keep the assessment bounded. The objective is not to rate an entire institution in the abstract, but to assess one recognizable operating environment or organizational slice.

Step 2 — Rate core institutional conditions

These questions assess whether the institution is converting structure into sustained performance, rather than relying on extra effort, workarounds, or temporary compensation.

Execution Coherence

How reliably does leadership intent translate into execution?

Priority Clarity

How clearly are priorities understood across the assessed environment?

Decision Reliability

How consistently do teams know who decides and who acts?

Follow-through

How effectively does the institution follow through once decisions are made?

Adaptive Capacity

How well does the institution maintain performance under changing conditions?

Institutional Confidence

How much confidence do people appear to have in the system they work inside?

Problem Resolution

How often do recurring problems actually get resolved rather than resurfacing?

Execution Stability

How evenly does performance hold across similar teams, units, or situations?

Step 3 — Mark recurring performance signals

Select the signals that most often describe the environment being assessed.
