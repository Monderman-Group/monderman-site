About

The difference between delivery and drift is measurable.

Monderman measures the structural conditions that determine whether institutions sustain their capacity or quietly lose it — surfacing drift before it becomes drag, and showing what to do once it has. Built by someone who has both studied these conditions and worked inside them.

Jason Adamson

Founder

Jason built Monderman after twenty years of working inside institutions where administrative reality determines whether the mission gets delivered. He started as an intelligence analyst at NSA, where he saw how administrative architecture inside a mature institution either supports or absorbs the work it was built to enable. He joined CrowdStrike during its growth phase as a senior threat intelligence analyst and analytic support team lead, where he watched the conditions for capacity or drag get established as the company scaled. He currently serves as a senior all-source analyst supporting U.S. Special Operations Command Europe (SOCEUR), where governance, tempo, and operating reality intersect daily.

He holds a Master of Science in Organization Development from Pepperdine University and a Bachelor of Arts in Russian and Eurasian Studies from the University of Texas at Austin. The frameworks behind Monderman are documented in a forthcoming book from Routledge. The platform is built for operators — chiefs of staff, division heads, program leads, transformation officers — who need to see what is actually slowing their organization and act on it.

What Monderman measures is not how people feel about their work. It is the structural conditions that determine whether work gets done — and whether the institution can sustain that capacity as conditions change.

Why This Platform

The market measures sentiment. Monderman measures the system.

Most platforms in the organizational health space measure how people feel about their work — engagement scores, culture indices, sentiment trends. Monderman measures the structural conditions that produce those feelings in the first place: where authority is clear and where it is ambiguous, where decisions move quickly and where they stall, where administrative systems accelerate work and where they restrict it.

The platform runs four diagnostics. Each one measures a different structural condition and produces a different operational outcome.

  • Structural Clarity Confident action
  • Decision Velocity Sustained advantage
  • Operational Systems Reclaimed capacity
  • Institutional Performance Organizational credibility

Practitioner Principles

Commitments that govern how this platform operates.

  • Diagnostic data stays in your environment. Nothing is stored on Monderman servers.
  • Results are read by humans, not just rendered by software. Synthesis is the work the platform exists to do.
  • No client logo will appear on this site without explicit, written permission.
  • The platform is built to be re-run. Value compounds with longitudinal use, and the architecture reflects that.
