Operational Systems Snapshot

A five-step diagnostic focused on operational friction. Use it to estimate where systems load is accumulating, which forms of friction are most prominent, and where structural adjustment may yield the fastest gains.

Use note: Do not enter confidential, classified, regulated, or personally sensitive information. This tool provides directional estimates, not audited operational values.

Step 1 — Define the operating context

Start with one team, unit, or recurring workflow. The goal is not to map the entire institution, but to assess one real area where operational friction may be slowing movement.

Keep this general. Avoid proprietary, personal, or otherwise sensitive details.

Step 2 — Assess decision friction

These items focus on approvals, ownership, waiting behavior, and how quickly decisions convert into action.

Decision Friction

Important decisions require too many approvals before action can begin.

Decision Friction

Decisions regularly stall because ownership or authority is unclear.

Decision Friction

Teams wait too long for senior validation before moving on straightforward issues.
Please respond to all three questions before continuing.

Step 3 — Assess coordination friction

These items focus on handoffs, duplication, meeting load, and whether work travels cleanly across functions.

Coordination Friction

Cross-functional work slows down because too many handoffs are required.

Coordination Friction

Different teams duplicate work because coordination and visibility are weak.

Coordination Friction

Meetings often substitute for clear execution pathways or ownership decisions.
Please respond to all three questions before continuing.

Step 4 — Assess administrative and adaptive load

These items focus on reporting load, process weight, rigidity, and the ease with which the operating system can respond to changing conditions.

Administrative Load

Reporting, documentation, or status upkeep consumes too much productive time.

Administrative Load

Internal process requirements often outweigh their operational value.

Adaptive Load

It is difficult to change process quickly, even when current methods are clearly slowing performance.

Adaptive Load

The organization reacts too slowly to meaningful shifts in demand, risk, or operating conditions.
Please respond to all four questions before continuing.
