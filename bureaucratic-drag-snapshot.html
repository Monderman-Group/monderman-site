Operational Systems Diagnostic

A focused diagnostic for surfacing where process weight, control burden, reporting load, and systems friction are absorbing operational effort.

What it surfaces

Process weight, systems switching, duplicate entry, reporting burden, exception handling load, and low-value control friction.

What you get back

An Operational Systems Score, directional benchmark comparison, burden charts, cost estimates, an executive-style summary, and a bridge into deeper advisory review.

Diagnostic Input

Assess one workflow, process environment, or operating system.

Use the guided diagnostic for the cleanest experience, or upload a template-compatible CSV if you already have structured workflow or process-burden data.

Step 1 · Operating context

Describe one recurring workflow, process environment, or operating system leaders care about.

This note is optional. Keep it short and non-sensitive.

This tool works best when focused on one process environment at a time.

Step 2 · Process load

This section measures how much procedural weight the workflow carries before work can move.

This tool is not measuring ownership or decision rights directly; it is measuring process weight and operating burden.

Step 3 · Systems friction

This section examines how many tools, systems, and manual workarounds the workflow depends on.

Where switching, re-entry, and manual patching rise, the operating system is usually carrying hidden weight.

Step 4 · Controls and reporting burden

This section measures how much compliance, reporting, and control activity is accumulating around the work.

This is where administrative burden often becomes normalized and starts to look like just how work gets done.

Step 5 · Optional estimates and privacy

These fields improve directional estimates of administrative burden, cost exposure, and operational drag.

      How this works

      This diagnostic evaluates one workflow or process environment at a time. It translates signals about process weight, systems friction, and administrative burden into a directional readout of operational drag.

      Best use cases

      • Partner or vendor onboarding
      • Program administration
      • Shared-service workflows
      • Compliance-heavy operating environments
      • Processes with repeated manual workarounds

      What this is not

      • Not a decision-rights tool
      • Not a team-structure tool
      • Not a productivity monitor
      • Not a replacement for workflow mapping

      What to trust

      Trust the burden pattern, not false precision. The strongest value here is surfacing where the operating system has become heavier than the work it is supposed to support.
