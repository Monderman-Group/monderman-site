ABM MVP

Decision Velocity Diagnostic

A lightweight diagnostic for identifying where decisions slow, why they slow, and where leaders should intervene first.

What it surfaces

Approval burden, handoff drag, ownership ambiguity, rework loops, escalation dependence, and workaround pressure.

What you get back

A Decision Velocity Score, friction flags, a plain-English interpretation, and recommended intervention priorities.

Diagnostic Input

Assess one decision process.

Use the guided diagnostic for the cleanest experience, or upload a template-compatible CSV if you already have structured decision-process data.

Decision context

Describe one decision process leaders care about: vendor approvals, hiring actions, budget reviews, policy decisions, or similar.

Timing and throughput

This helps distinguish normal complexity from accumulated decision burden.

Approval and handoff burden

These are common sources of delay that leaders can often act on directly.

Coordination and ownership

This captures the friction that tends to hide behind “normal coordination.”

Rework, workarounds, and review burden

This is where compensatory behavior and hidden drag often start to appear.

This diagnostic is directional. It is designed to produce a credible first read on decision latency, not an audited enterprise measurement.

          How this works

          This MVP evaluates one decision process at a time. It translates a small set of structured signals into a directional readout of decision velocity, friction, and likely intervention points.

          Best use cases

          • Vendor approvals
          • Hiring and staffing decisions
          • Budget and funding reviews
          • Policy or exception approvals
          • Program decisions requiring multiple stakeholders

          What this is not

          • Not an enterprise deployment
          • Not a compliance tool
          • Not a productivity monitor
          • Not a replacement for formal process analysis

          Next step

          If the readout resonates, the next conversation is usually about a guided pilot using real decision-process data and a narrower organizational context.
