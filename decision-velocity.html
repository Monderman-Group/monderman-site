Operational

Built for people closest to the work. Best for surfacing where decisions stall, where manual follow-up is needed, and where the pathway depends on workarounds, rework, or institutional memory.

Managerial

Built for people coordinating teams and execution. Best for identifying recurring approval burden, escalation patterns, coordination drag, and where pathway weight is increasing over time.

Executive

Built for leaders shaping structure, governance, and risk posture. Best for assessing control climate, approval accumulation, structural drift, and the relationship between governance weight and execution responsiveness.

Choose diagnostic depth

10 minutes
A faster directional read for initial signal detection, quick baselining, or a bounded first pass. Lighter evidence base, still useful.
30 minutes
A stronger diagnostic with deeper branching, better contradiction detection, and more grounded qualitative and quantitative signal.
60 minutes
The deepest self-guided version. Adds trajectory, surrounding-context, and repeat-use signal without filler.
Shorter does not mean bad. Longer runs simply capture more structural signal and support better opportunity framing over time.

Why time depth matters

  • Baseline once: get a first read on the pathway as it exists today
  • Reassess later: rerun after redesign, staffing change, new controls, or environmental shift
  • Compare perspectives: use the same pathway across participant levels to see where signal diverges
  • Track drift: longer runs pick up trajectory, surrounding conditions, and accumulating burden more clearly
