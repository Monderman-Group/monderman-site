Monderman diagnostic canvas

Decision Velocity

Diagnose where decisions slow, what structural conditions are producing that slowdown, and where leadership should intervene first. This version is built as a branching institutional interpretation layer — not a static survey.

What it surfaces

Approval burden, coordination load, decision-rights ambiguity, escalation dependence, workaround pressure, handoff drag, and structural contradiction signals.

What you get back

A Monderman decision velocity score, route family, benchmark position, annual time and cost exposure, capacity drag estimate, and executive interpretation.

Designed for repeated use

Run by process, team, function, business unit, or time period. Each run can feed later comparison and cross-tool synthesis.

Entry state

A live institutional interpretation layer.

This diagnostic evaluates one decision pathway at a time. It does not ask every user the same questions in the same order. Instead, it opens the next most relevant branch based on the dominant signal you surface first.

Estimated time

Most runs take 5–8 minutes. Some pathways are shorter; some open deeper because the pattern warrants it.

Best for

Vendor approvals, staffing decisions, budget reviews, program routing, exception handling, and policy-heavy pathways.

Interaction logic

Answers compress into prior cards, new branches open laterally, and the system builds a visible interpretation path as it learns.

Privacy

Do not enter classified, regulated, or sensitive information. Short operational descriptions are enough for directional analysis.

What changed

This version replaces the static step-survey with a branching canvas. It is designed to feel like a serious operating layer rather than a generic diagnostic wizard.

What to trust

Trust the pattern, the route family, and the exposure logic. The value is in the structured interpretation of the pathway, not false precision for its own sake.

Subscription logic

  • Run once for a quick read on a single pathway
  • Run repeatedly across functions or business units
  • Track recurring decision drag over time
  • Feed later synthesis across tools
