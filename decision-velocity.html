Decision Velocity Snapshot

A five-step diagnostic focused on flow. Use it to estimate where decisions are slowing, how much delay is accumulating, and which design conditions are most likely creating drag.

Use note: Do not enter confidential, classified, regulated, or personally sensitive information. This tool provides directional estimates, not audited operational values.

Step 1 — Define the decision flow

Start with one recurring decision pathway. Think about a real decision that should move faster than it currently does.

Step 2 — Map the movement

Now estimate how the decision actually moves. Focus on approvals, cycle time, and wait time rather than role clarity.

Step 3 — Identify where the flow stalls

Select the patterns that best describe where decisions slow down. This tool is looking for latency, queue buildup, and repeated stop points.

Step 4 — Assess escalation and decision pressure

Decision velocity problems are often less about one approval and more about how often decisions escalate, bounce, or wait for senior attention.

Step 5 — Estimated decision drag

These results are directional. They are designed to help make hidden delay visible and show where approval design, escalation patterns, and queue effects are likely slowing movement through the decision flow.
